RG Kar verdict: Sanjay Roy held guilty of rape and murder of doctor

The term of the sentence which can be from death to life imprisonment will be pronounced on January 20 Updated - January 18, 2025 05:56 pm IST - Kolkata.

Shiv Sahay Singh, Shrabana Chatterjee,Moyurie Som Scene outside Sealdah Court where the verdict in R. G. Kar rape and murder was pronounced The Additional District and Sessions Court, Sealdah on (January 18, 2025) held accused Sanjay Roy “guilty” of rape and murder of an on-duty doctor at the state-run R.G. Kar Medical College and Hospital.

Judge Anirban Das pronounced the verdict in the presence of the accused. The parents of the victim were also present in the court premises. The term of the sentence will be pronounced on Monday (January 20).

The Court said that it finds the accused guilty on the basis of evidence put forward by the Central Bureau of India (CBI) for which the highest punishment will be death and least will be life imprisonment. The judge said the accused is being held guilty of Sections 64, 66, and 103 (1) of Bharatiya Nyay Samhita. The Judge informed Sanjay Roy that he had entered the hospital on August 9 and smothered the victim to death and committed sexual assault on her.

Kolkata doctor rape and murder case: The complete coverage. The CBI filed a chargesheet against Sanjay Roy, a civic police volunteer, on October 7. The victim’s body was recovered from a seminar room in a hospital on August 9. Roy, who had been arrested by the Kolkata Police before the case was transferred to the CBI, is accused of involvement in the crime. The CBI’s chargesheet listed 11 pieces of evidence confirming his role in the crime, including CCTV footage that shows him entering and exiting the scene.

Sanjay Roy pleaded his innocence when the verdict was read out in the court. “I did not do this, sir. The ones who have done this, why are they not being held?,” he told the court. The Judge said that Roy would get a chance to speak on Monday before the sentencing.
